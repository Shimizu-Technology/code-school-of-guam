# Code School of Guam - Frequently Asked Questions

## General Questions

### What is Code School of Guam?
Code School of Guam (CSG) is Guam's first AI-native coding bootcamp. We teach complete beginners to become full-stack web developers who can build AI-powered applications. Our program covers Ruby, Rails, React, Python, and AI Engineering in under 6 months.

### Who is this for?
Anyone interested in learning to code — no prior experience needed! Our students include career changers, recent graduates, professionals looking to add tech skills, and anyone curious about building software and AI applications. You just need a laptop, internet, and motivation.

### Is there an age requirement?
There's no upper age limit. For students under 18, we require parental consent. Our students range from high schoolers to professionals in their 50s changing careers. Everyone learns together and supports each other. There's no "right age" to learn to code — just the right motivation.

### Do I need any coding experience?
No! We start from absolute zero. The 5-week pre-work phase builds your foundation before live classes begin. By the end, you'll be building full-stack applications with AI features.

### How long is the program?
22 weeks total: 5 weeks of pre-work + 17 weeks of live instruction. That's about 5.5 months from start to finish.

## Format & Schedule

### What is the hybrid format?
Our hybrid format combines three types of learning:
- **Live Classes (Tue & Thu, 6–9 PM ChST)**: Your instructor teaches new concepts via Zoom with live coding demos, exercises, and Q&A.
- **Guided Practice (Mon, Wed, Fri)**: Structured async days with lesson recordings, required exercises, and instructor feedback. You choose when during the day to complete them.
- **1-on-1 Mentorship (Saturday)**: Individual sessions with the Lead Instructor to review your progress and work through blockers.

This format gives you the guided structure of a traditional bootcamp with the flexibility to learn at your own pace on async days.

### Are classes in-person or online?
Classes are online via Zoom. If all students in a cohort agree, we may add optional in-person sessions — but online is the default and works great.

### What's the weekly time commitment?
About 13.5 hours per week: 6 hours of live instruction (Tue/Thu) plus about 7.5 hours of structured async practice (Mon/Wed/Fri). Saturday 1-on-1s are an additional 30-60 minutes.

### What about the pre-work phase?
Pre-work is also hybrid: live sessions Tuesday & Thursday 6:00–7:30 PM (shorter than main class since content is foundational), Saturday 1-on-1s, and async exercises on Mon/Wed/Fri. Pre-work covers programming basics, terminal, Git, and environment setup.

## Curriculum & AI

### What AI tools will I learn?
You'll learn to use Cursor IDE (Pro license included), ChatGPT, the OpenAI API, Pinecone vector databases, and modern AI engineering patterns like RAG systems, prompt engineering, function calling, and AI agents. Our 6-phase AI progression starts with no AI tools and gradually builds to you creating AI-powered applications from scratch.

### Do I need to know anything about AI before starting?
Not at all! We teach AI from scratch with a progressive approach. You start with coding fundamentals without any AI tools (Weeks 1-3), then gradually learn to use AI as a study buddy, debugging partner, and coding accelerator. By Weeks 10-12, you're building chatbots and RAG systems. No prior AI knowledge required.

### What's the AI-as-a-Tool Philosophy?
We don't ban AI — we teach you to use it intelligently. Think of it like math class: you learn multiplication tables before using a calculator. Our 6-phase approach ensures you understand the fundamentals before leveraging AI to accelerate your work. By graduation, you'll be an AI-native developer who uses AI as a superpower, not a crutch.

### What programming languages will I learn?
Ruby, JavaScript, Python, HTML, CSS, and SQL. You'll also learn frameworks like Ruby on Rails, React, and Tailwind CSS, plus AI tools like the OpenAI API and Pinecone.

### What's Cursor IDE?
Cursor is an AI-powered code editor built on top of VS Code. It suggests code as you type, helps you debug, and can answer questions about your codebase. We include a Cursor Pro license for all students starting in Week 4. It's the same tool professional developers use to build software faster.
