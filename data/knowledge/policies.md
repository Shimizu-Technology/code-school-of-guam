# Code School of Guam - Policies

## Attendance Policy

Attendance is crucial for your success in the program.

### Requirements
- Students are expected to attend all scheduled classes punctually
- Classes are held Monday through Thursday, 6:00pm - 9:00pm (Guam Time)

### Unexcused Absences
- Missing more than three unexcused classes may result in dismissal from the program without a refund
- An unexcused absence is any absence without prior approval or valid documentation

### Excused Absences
- Excused absences are allowed for situations beyond your control (e.g., medical emergencies, family emergencies)
- Documentation may be required after three excused absences
- Please notify the instructor as soon as possible if you need to miss a class

## Code of Conduct

We are committed to providing a respectful and inclusive learning environment.

### Expected Behavior
- Treat all individuals with respect and courtesy
- Participate constructively in discussions and group work
- Support fellow students in their learning journey
- Maintain professional communication at all times

### Prohibited Behavior
- Discrimination of any kind
- Harassment or inappropriate behavior
- Disruptive conduct that interferes with learning
- Violations may result in disciplinary action, up to dismissal without a refund

## Refund Policy

### Non-Refundable Deposit
- A $500 non-refundable deposit is required to secure your enrollment
- This deposit is applied toward your total tuition

### Refund Eligibility
- **Before Week 2**: Full tuition (minus the $500 deposit) is refundable if you withdraw before the start of the second week of classes
- **Week 2 and Beyond**: No refunds will be issued from the second week onward

### Dismissal
- Students dismissed due to violations of the Code of Conduct or failure to meet program requirements are not eligible for a refund

## Academic Integrity

### Original Work
- Students must submit original work unless collaboration is explicitly permitted
- Properly attribute any code or resources used from external sources

### Prohibited Activities
- Cheating on assignments or assessments
- Plagiarism - presenting others' work as your own
- Unauthorized assistance on individual assignments
- Sharing solutions with other students for individual work

### Consequences
- Violations of academic integrity may result in disciplinary action
- Serious violations may lead to dismissal from the program without a refund
