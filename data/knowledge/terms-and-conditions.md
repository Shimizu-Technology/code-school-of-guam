# Code School of Guam - Terms and Conditions

By enrolling in the Code School of Guam, you agree to the following terms and conditions. Please review these carefully before confirming your participation.

---

## 1. Attendance Policy

Attendance is critical to your success in the program. Students are expected to attend all scheduled classes punctually. Missing more than three unexcused classes may result in dismissal from the program without a refund. Excused absences (e.g., medical emergencies) will be allowed with appropriate documentation after three excused absences.

---

## 2. Admissions Communication

After submitting your application, you will receive email notifications detailing the next steps. This includes scheduling an interview, payment instructions for the deposit, and onboarding information. Be sure to check your email regularly to stay informed.

---

## 3. Code of Conduct

We are committed to maintaining a respectful and inclusive learning environment. All students are expected to treat their peers, instructors, and staff with courtesy and respect. Any form of discrimination, harassment, or inappropriate behavior will not be tolerated and may result in disciplinary action, including dismissal from the program without a refund.

---

## 4. Refund Policy

- A **non-refundable deposit of $500** is required to secure your enrollment in the program.
- **Full tuition (minus the deposit)** is refundable if you withdraw before the start of the second week of live classes.
- **After the second week of live classes**, no refunds will be issued.
- Students dismissed due to violations of the Code of Conduct or failure to meet program requirements are not eligible for a refund.

---

## 5. Installment Payment Terms

### Commitment to Pay
By choosing the installment plan, you agree to pay the full tuition amount of $10,000, regardless of your continued participation in the program.

### Payment Schedule
- The first installment payment is due by the Monday before pre-work begins.
- Subsequent payments are due on the first Monday of each month until the end of the class.

### Consequences of Non-Payment
- If a payment is not made by the due date, a **3-day grace period** will be provided.
- If payment is not received by the end of the grace period, you will be **removed from the program**, and access to all course materials will be revoked.
- Re-entry into the program will only be allowed upon full payment of any outstanding balance.
- Continued failure to pay may result in the outstanding balance being **sent to collections**, which could impact your credit.

### No Refund for Partial Completion
If you withdraw or are dismissed after the second week of live classes, you are still responsible for paying the remaining balance of your tuition, and no refunds will be provided for partial completion of the course.

---

## 6. Academic Integrity

All work submitted must be your own unless collaboration is explicitly permitted. Plagiarism, cheating, or unauthorized assistance will not be tolerated and may result in dismissal from the program without a refund.

### Note on AI Tools
Using AI assistants (GitHub Copilot, ChatGPT, etc.) is permitted and encouraged as part of learning modern development practices. However, you must understand and be able to explain any code you submit.

---

## 7. Privacy and Data Protection

We respect your privacy. The personal information you provide during enrollment and coursework is used solely for educational and administrative purposes. By enrolling in the Code School of Guam, you consent to the collection and use of your data as described in our Privacy and Data Protection policy.

---

## 8. Technology Requirements

To participate in the program, students must have access to a reliable computer (preferably a Mac) and a stable high-speed internet connection. While we welcome students using different operating systems, using a Mac will provide the most seamless experience. You are responsible for ensuring your computer meets the minimum technical specifications outlined in the course materials.

---

## 9. Program Commitment

This program requires a substantial time commitment:

- **Pre-work (5 weeks)**: Self-paced with weekly check-ins, approximately 10-15 hours per week
- **Live Class (17 weeks)**: Approximately 17.5 hours of live class time per week (Mon-Fri evenings), plus an additional 5-10 hours of study and assignments

By enrolling, you confirm that you are ready to dedicate the necessary time and effort to successfully complete the course.

---

## 10. Career Services and Internship

We offer career services and an optional internship as part of the program. Participation in the internship is voluntary and subject to meeting certain eligibility criteria. These services are designed to help you transition into a software development career, but employment outcomes are not guaranteed.
